About Us

A festival that brings music, connection, and social impact together.

Who We Are

After Tomorrow is an initiative from the NGO Harmony for Tomorrow, a non-profit that believes culture can build real bridges between people. Our organization works with volunteers, local partners, and artists who want to use music to create connection, mental calm, and social inclusion.

We are not a traditional festival organizer that only builds a stage. We develop projects where art, education, and community spirit strengthen one another. That is why this festival feels like a place to meet: accessible, welcoming, and open to different ages and backgrounds.

Why This Festival?

The idea grew out of a series of volunteer projects in schools and community centers. We kept seeing how often young people and families needed a safe place where they could breathe, listen, and reconnect with others. Music quickly proved to be the easiest starting point: you do not need perfect words to feel understood.

That is why we are organizing a festival in Tirol, Austria. The natural setting supports our goal of creating space for calm, reflection, and togetherness. Not a massive, distant event, but one where people genuinely meet one another.

From Idea to Festival

It started small, with a benefit night for peace education. The response was so positive that we began to ask: what if we created a full festival that captured the same feeling, but on a larger, more inclusive, and longer-lasting scale?

Why This Kind of Festival?

Music festivals bring together people who would not normally meet. By combining concerts with workshops, discussion tables, and family-friendly activities, the event becomes more than entertainment. It becomes a shared experience with meaning.

Our Promise

A portion of the proceeds goes to local youth and peace projects. That way, the festival is not only a memorable day, but also an investment in community, creativity, and trust between people.
