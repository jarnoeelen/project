Family Friendly

After Tomorrow is built for all ages — from toddlers to grandparents.

A Festival for Every Generation

We designed After Tomorrow so families can enjoy it together without compromise. There is space for young children to play safely, quiet corners for those who need a rest, and a full programme that works for every age alongside the main stage.

Our family zone is a short walk from the main stage — close enough to hear the music, calm enough to let a toddler nap on a blanket while parents listen.

Family Spaces & Options

Family Zone

A large dedicated area on the grass with hay bales, picnic blankets, and natural shade from the alpine trees. Positioned with a clear view of the main stage so you never miss a set.

Kids Corner

An enclosed, supervised activity space open on both days from 10:00 to 17:00. Expect face painting, balloon art, drawing, and nature crafts. Supervised by trained staff. Free for all children under 14.

Quiet Space Tent

A calm, shaded tent away from the main stage for families who need a quieter moment. Ideal for naps, sensory breaks, and breastfeeding. Open all day, no booking required.

Practical Facilities

  • Nursing room: A private, comfortable room available inside the venue at all times.
  • Buggy parking: Free, secure storage for prams and pushchairs near the main entrance.
  • Accessible toilets: Including baby-changing facilities across the site.
  • Family meals: All food trucks offer child-friendly portion sizes and allergen information on request.
  • Lost children point: Clearly signposted at the main information desk near the entrance.
  • First aid: A dedicated family first aid station is open throughout both festival days.

Family Tickets

We offer a Family Pack that covers two adults and two children (under 12) at a reduced combined rate. Children under 5 enter free when accompanied by a ticketed adult.

All children must stay with a parent or responsible adult at all times. Children 12 and under can purchase a discounted Day Pass at half the standard price.

Good to Know

The festival grounds are fully flat and accessible, with wide paths suitable for buggies and wheelchairs throughout. There are no stairs or barriers between any of the main areas.

The noise level near the family zone is managed carefully — it is loud enough to feel the music, but not so loud that it is overwhelming for young children. Ear protection for children is available free at the information desk.

If you have specific needs or questions before you arrive, reach out via the Contact page and our team will help.
